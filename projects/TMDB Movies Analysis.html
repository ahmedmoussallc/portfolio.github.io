Udacity investigate a Dataset Project¶

For this project, I chose the TMDB movies dataset.

After data cleaning we're going to answer some questions, to better explain the dataset.

Questions:¶

  • 1 - What is happening to the movie rating performance over the years?
  • 2 - Is the movie business making more profit over the years or less?
  • 3 - Are the number of movies increasing per year?
  • 4 - What's the distribution of the movies runtime values?
  • 5 - What's the relationship between budget spent on a movie and it's popularity?
  • 6 - Finally, what's the relationship between budget spent and profit acquired?

In [17]:

df.groupby(by = "release_year")["vote_average"].mean().plot();
plt.xlabel("Movie Release Year")
plt.ylabel("Averge Vote")
plt.title("Voting Average Over The Years")
plt.show()

As we can see from the line chart above, the voting average seems to be going down over the years

2 - Is the movie business making more profit over the years or less?¶

In [18]:
# In order to do this
# we need to create a new column, let's call it profit:

df["profit"] = df.revenue - df.budget
In [19]:
# let's also create an adjusted profit column:

df["profit_adj"] = df.revenue_adj - df.budget_adj

Conclusions:¶

  • The voting average seems to be going down over the years
  • The movie business is making less profit over the years
  • The number of movies released increase on a yearly basis
  • Most of the movies have duration between 85 and 125 minutes.
  • Minimum movie duration is 63 min.
  • Maximum movie duration is 201 min.
  • Average movie duration is 110 min.
  • 25% of the movies have a duration of less than 97 min.
  • 50% of the movies have a duration of less than 107 min.
  • 75% of the movies have a duration of less than 121 min.
  • Budget and popularity both seem to have a positive linear relationship, up to a certain point where we can consider the diminishing returns.
  • Budget and profit both seem to have a positive linear relationship, up to a certain point where we can consider the diminishing returns.

Limitations:¶

  • The dataset has a lot of missing values
  • The dataset has a lot of zero values
  • There are some outliers in the runtime column
  • Some columns had a wrong data type
